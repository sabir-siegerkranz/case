Stage 1
Awareness
How the bank enters the sole proprietor's consideration set
 Pre-existing private account relationship; local brand omnipresence; referrals from Handwerker networks and trade associations; Sparkasse-sponsored Gründer events at regional level.
passive pull
Sole proprietors rarely actively choose — they extend an existing private account into business use. The bank is inherited, not selected. Active competitor comparison almost never happens at this stage.
 Multi-generational trade relationships concentrated in rural areas; referrals from Handwerkskammer and cooperative networks; cooperative membership culture in craft and artisan sectors.
community pull
Visibility is structurally limited outside rural and trade contexts. Urban Freiberufler rarely encounter Volksbank organically — the bank does not advertise for this segment at national scale.
 National advertising campaigns; Unternehmerperspektiven study coverage in business press; comparison platform listings; dedicated Gründer discount campaigns.
brand awareness
Discovery via comparison platforms frequently leads to fee sticker shock before the value of relationship banking is understood. Awareness does not automatically convert to positive evaluation.
 Deutsche Bank: heritage brand and international reputation drives passive awareness. FYRST: strong SEO presence on Geschäftskonto comparison platforms; "kostenlos für Freiberufler" messaging immediately legible.
dual-brand complexity
Many prospective customers unclear whether they are banking with Deutsche Bank, FYRST, or Postbank. The three-brand architecture creates confusion at first contact and reduces perceived institutional clarity.
 Cross-sell from 9 million existing private customers; strong SEO coverage for Gründer and Freiberufler queries; established Direktbank brand trust from private banking context.
trusted pull
Business banking launched September 2024 — limited organic word-of-mouth at time of writing. Business-specific marketing campaigns still maturing. Many existing ING private customers unaware the business product exists.
Stage 2
Consideration & research
Fee comparison and evaluation before deciding
 Comparison platform reviews; conversation with regional branch advisor; peer referrals. Regional fee variation makes systematic online comparison difficult — no national price page.
mixed
No nationally consistent pricing means fee research requires contacting or visiting the specific local institution. A Sparkasse in Munich may price differently from one in Hamburg for identical services.
 Fee structures not publicly standardised across approximately 659 independent institutions. No national comparison page exists. Must request information from the local branch or call ahead before any fee can be confirmed.
friction
Inability to obtain a fee quote without a branch visit is a structural barrier vs. digital-first competitors. The research phase is unusually opaque relative to every other player reviewed.
 Online fee calculator and clear three-tier structure (Klassik / Premium / PremiumPlus) publicly available. Gründer introductory pricing on Premium (€8.90 for 24 months) is prominently communicated.
mixed
Comparison platforms consistently flag high base fees versus neobank alternatives. Positive evaluation requires the prospect to already believe in relationship banking value — a belief less common among younger Freiberufler.
 Deutsche Bank: wide product suite creates decision complexity for simple sole-proprietor use cases. FYRST: clean one-page pricing; free tier for Freiberufler immediately legible on comparison platforms.
FYRST clear
FYRST's V-Pay card limitations, the Schufa check requirement, and the three-way Deutsche Bank / Postbank / FYRST back-end are not visible at the research stage — discovered only post-onboarding.
 Pricing page transparent; Bestpreis-Garantie prominently explained; 6-month or 12-month (founders) free period clearly stated upfront. Strong presence on comparison platforms from launch.
positive
As a new product, long-term independent user reviews are thin at the point of research. Prospective customers cannot yet rely on a large community of testimonials to corroborate the bank's self-presentation.
Stage 3
Onboarding & account opening
From decision to a fully functional account
 Online application possible at some regional institutions; identity verification via Video-Ident, Post-Ident, or in-branch visit (varies by institution). Card and PIN dispatched by post separately. Typical timeline: 1–2 weeks.
mixed
Branch-only verification still required at many Sparkassen. Card and PIN arrive separately by post — first card use is delayed. Timeline varies widely and is not predictable during research.
 In-person branch appointment mandatory at nearly all Volksbanken — no online account opening path exists. Required documents: ID, Steuernummer, Gewerbeanmeldung where applicable. Account typically active within a few days of the branch visit.
friction
Sole proprietors must take time off work to visit a branch during business hours. No digital alternative is available regardless of how simple the entity structure. The most restrictive onboarding process of any player reviewed.
 Online account opening available for Freiberufler and Einzelunternehmer; Video-Ident supported. GmbH and UG still require a branch visit. The Gründer introductory pricing is communicated at the point of application.
mixed
Online process involves multiple document uploads with limited real-time status feedback — customers often unsure whether their application is progressing. Legal entities beyond sole traders must still visit a branch.
 Deutsche Bank: branch appointment required for main account products. FYRST: fully digital; Schufa check conducted upfront; stated timeline a few business days. Documented issue: conflicts between FYRST, Postbank, and Deutsche Bank systems during setup; BestSign app authentication failures reported.
FYRST fragile
Trustpilot reviews document accounts inaccessible for days immediately after opening due to BestSign authentication failures and IT system conflicts across the three-entity architecture. The worst onboarding experience of the five banks for early adopters.
 Fully digital via Video-Ident or Post-Ident; stated time approximately 20 minutes. Account accessible online immediately after verification. Debit card dispatched by post afterward.
positive
Minimum 6 months of trading history required (waived for first-year founders). GbR and UG are excluded from eligibility entirely. Debit card still requires postal delivery — not instant as with neobanks like Qonto or N26.
Stage 4
Daily banking usage
Routine payments, transfers, card use, account management
 Full-featured online banking (desktop and Sparkasse Business app); instant SEPA available; nationwide ATM network free of charge; per-transaction fees apply (approx. €1.29 per outgoing SEPA transfer at many institutions).
mixed
Per-transaction fees accumulate materially for users with more than 20–30 transactions per month. Online banking reliability criticised in reviews. No sub-accounts included by default. No spending analytics. No invoicing tool.
 VR-Banking app for mobile and desktop; beleghafte (paper-based) transactions supported at branches; free ATM withdrawals within the cooperative network; DATEV-export accessible via EBICS at most institutions.
mixed
Digital experience lags competitors. Per-transaction fees apply at most institutions. No real-time push notifications at most Volksbanken. Branch still expected for document-based transactions — not practical for digitally-native Freiberufler.
 Online banking rated above other incumbents on usability (app rated 7/10 in independent reviews); 10 free postings per month in Klassik; €0.20 per posting thereafter; Cash Group ATMs free of charge.
mixed
10 free postings exhausted quickly by active Freiberufler sending invoices weekly. No sub-accounts, no spending analytics, no invoicing. App described as "basic" compared with modern fintechs — lacks features competitors offer at lower prices.
 FYRST: 50 free beleglose Buchungen per month; Cash Group ATMs free; no Apple Pay or Google Pay on V-Pay Girocard; no sub-accounts included in either tier; no in-app invoicing.
V-Pay friction
FYRST's V-Pay Girocard fails on many international websites and online merchants. Mastercard Debit alternative costs extra. Multiple Trustpilot reviews cite the card failing randomly abroad and during online purchases — one reviewer uses the account solely for its DATEV interface despite these failures.
 Instant SEPA transfers 24/7; 3 free ATM withdrawals per month; Visa Business Debit accepted across European merchants; built-in Rechnungsmanager for invoicing and receipt capture; up to 5 sub-accounts with individual IBANs included free.
positive
No non-SEPA international transfers — excludes internationally active sole proprietors entirely. No Kontokorrentkredit or overdraft. PSD2-only accounting link — no native DATEV integration. Some roadmap features (e.g. B2B Lastschrift) still pending at time of writing.
Stage 5
Accounting & tax workflow
DATEV, Steuerberater handoff, VAT returns, receipt management
 DATEV-compatible data export via EBICS or banking interface available at most institutions — requires setup with a branch advisor. No native invoicing. No VAT tracking. Manual CSV or PDF statement export remains the default workflow for most sole proprietors.
mixed
Real-time DATEV sync requires additional manual configuration steps. No built-in receipt capture. No invoice tool. Interface availability and quality varies significantly by regional institution — not a standardised feature.
 DATEV export via EBICS or banking interface available at most Volksbanken. No native invoicing. No VAT tracking. Monthly statement export manually initiated in most cases.
mixed
Same structural gaps as Sparkasse. Some institutions require a branch interaction to configure the accounting interface. No receipt capture, no VAT calculation, no invoicing tool — the complete accounting workflow remains external.
 DATEV interface accessible via Commerzbank banking software. Partner discount arrangements with sevDesk and Lexoffice exist but no product integration. No native invoicing. No VAT tracking or USt-VA support.
friction
Bureaucracy reduction is the #1 pain point for SMEs in Commerzbank's own 2025 Unternehmerperspektiven research. The bank's product does not address it. The full accounting workflow is outsourced to the Steuerberater or a third-party tool at additional cost.
 FYRST: interfaces with sevDesk, Lexoffice, and Sage via partner agreements with discounts; WISO EÜR-compatible export available. No in-app invoicing. No VAT tracking.
mixed
DATEV connection is PSD2-only — no DATEVconnect or EBICS direct integration. Praised by users when functioning; reliability tied to FYRST's underlying IT stability, which has been variable. One Trustpilot reviewer states: "I only use Fyrst because of my tax advisor, and it has a Schnittstelle to DATEV — when it works it is good."
 PSD2 interface connects to DATEV Unternehmen online, Lexware, and sevDesk. Built-in Rechnungsmanager supports invoice creation and receipt digitisation; receipt sharing with Steuerberater mentioned in product documentation. No native VAT calculation or USt-VA filing.
mixed
PSD2 link requires third-party tools to complete the Steuerberater-to-DATEV workflow — it is not a native integration. Manual setup steps required, as documented in ING FAQ. No VAT tracking or quarterly return support built in.
Stage 6
Customer support & advisor contact
Hotlines, branch advisors, email, live chat
 Dedicated personal advisor at the account-holding branch; phone hotline with callback service; live chat at some institutions; branch visit for complex matters. Advisory available only at the local Sparkasse — cannot use another branch while travelling.
positive (regional)
Advisor quality varies significantly by region and individual. Branch-only advisory means no access while away. Some advisors highly praised in reviews; others reported as unavailable or quietly replaced by call-centre arrangements.
 Dedicated business advisor at local branch; strong advisory reputation for Gründer support, KfW financing, and Fördermittel access; phone and email channels available. Consistently rated highly for SME advisory quality on comparison platforms.
positive
Branch-only model — no effective phone advisory without a pre-existing branch relationship at most institutions. No 24/7 or digital channel. Rural advisors consistently well-rated; urban experience more variable. Formal business hours only.
 Shared business advisory centre replaces dedicated advisors for small-account customers; phone Mon–Fri 08:00–20:00; email; online appointment booking. No assigned personal advisor for sole proprietors and micro-firms.
friction
The removal of dedicated personal advisors for small firms is the single most frequently cited complaint in published reviews. One review: "KATASTROPHE!!!! Und wenn ihr nicht eine Riesige Firma sind, die Hände weg!!!!" (20+ years as a customer). Shared advisory provides generic, transactional responses rather than relationship-based guidance.
 Deutsche Bank: branch advisor network; business advisory centre; phone Mon–Fri 08:00–20:00. FYRST: phone 08:00–17:00 Mon–Fri; email only; no branch. Both channels reported as difficult to reach during high-demand periods.
FYRST friction
FYRST phone cutoff at 17:00 called "not zeitgemäß" in reviews. Email response delays documented repeatedly. No weekend channel. No physical fallback when digital issues arise. Several Trustpilot reviews escalate to threats of BaFin complaint after no response to emails about blocked funds.
 Business Banking Support by phone Mon–Fri 09:00–18:00; email at business@ing.de; in-app secure message box; Neukunden-Hotline for onboarding queries. No branch — entirely digital and asynchronous.
mixed
ING's general customer service has a documented reputation for unresponsiveness: slow email replies, unreachable hotlines, and automated responses (Trustpilot and Finanzwissen reviews). Business-specific support channel is new and not yet independently reviewed at scale. Risk of the same culture applying to business customers.
Stage 7
Retention & loyalty
What keeps sole proprietors from switching
 Hausbank relationship; local financing access (Kontokorrentkredit, property loans, KfW credit); Gründer advisory continuity; community event participation. Adding a neobank Zweitkonto without closing the Sparkasse account is the dominant retention pattern.
high stickiness
Retention is relationship-based, not product-based — stickiness depends on credit facilities being in place. Price-sensitive sole proprietors with no credit need often quietly add a neobank Zweitkonto while maintaining the Sparkasse for inertia reasons.
 Cooperative membership and profit-sharing; multi-year personal advisor relationship; deep Mittelstand financing capability; KfW credit access via the local genossenschaftliche relationship.
high stickiness
Stickiness concentrated among established, financing-dependent businesses. Younger, digitally-native Freiberufler see no product compelling enough to prevent Zweitkonto migration to a neobank. No digital-only feature competes with neobank pricing.
 Brand familiarity; Premium plan 24-month introductory pricing creates switching inertia during the discount window; annual Unternehmerperspektiven study positions Commerzbank as an engaged SME partner.
mixed
Loss of personal advisor is a documented churn driver for small firms. After the 24-month introductory Premium period expires (€8.90 → €34.90/month), the price jump forces active re-evaluation of the full relationship — typically for the first time.
 Deutsche Bank: international reach, corporate credit products, Global Hausbank positioning for larger clients. FYRST: €0 base tier retains cost-sensitive users; sevDesk / Lexoffice accounting workflow partnerships create stickiness once integrated.
mixed
FYRST retention is fragile — dependent on IT stability. Trustpilot shows churn narratives closely following unresolved IT incidents. Deutsche Bank's strength in large corporate segments creates minimal retention value for solo Freiberufler who need none of those capabilities.
 Bestpreis-Garantie removes ongoing monthly price anxiety. Geschäftskredit tie-in creates stickiness once drawn upon. Business Extra-Konto (Tagesgeld) adds product depth. Private-to-business cross-sell is an asset not available to neobanks starting from scratch.
too early to assess
Product launched September 2024 — long-term retention patterns not yet observable. No dedicated account-switching service for the Geschäftskonto. No overdraft means actively growing businesses eventually encounter a capability ceiling and are pushed toward competitors.
Stage 8
Churn triggers & exit
Why customers leave or open a Zweitkonto elsewhere
 High per-transaction fees for high-volume users; expensive for digital-only workflows; absence of invoicing, native DATEV, and VAT tools. Opening a neobank Zweitkonto without fully closing the Sparkasse is the dominant exit pattern — partial churn rather than full exit.
partial churn
Full exit typically triggered only when a credit relationship ends, the business closes, or the owner relocates outside the branch's geographic coverage area. The Sparkasse rarely loses sole proprietors entirely — they are diluted, not departed.
 In-person-only model becomes untenable as the business operates more digitally; high fees relative to neobanks; mandatory branch visits for any account changes; complete absence of digital-only workflow support.
friction triggers
Churn most likely among digitally-native Freiberufler who resent mandatory branch visits and see no product merit in the relationship. Trade-sector SMEs with active financing relationships tend to stay, sometimes for decades.
 Post-24-month price jump (Premium: €8.90 → €34.90/month) is the most documented re-evaluation trigger. Removal of dedicated personal advisor for small accounts is the primary emotional churn driver, as evidenced by review platform sentiment.
price shock + service erosion
"Hände weg für kleine Firmen" is the documented user sentiment pattern in published reviews. Once the personal advisor is gone and the introductory price expires simultaneously, the premium cost of a Commerzbank account becomes unjustifiable against neobank alternatives for the sole-proprietor segment.
 FYRST: V-Pay card failures; IT integration conflicts between FYRST, Postbank, and Deutsche Bank systems; inaccessible customer support during critical periods. Users stay while the product works; they exit when a technical incident goes unresolved.
support failure churn
Documented trigger: SEPA transfer blocked over a Christmas holiday period; no support response for days; customer forced to borrow money personally and could not pay rent. The resulting Trustpilot review ("STAY AWAY FROM THIS BANK") is representative of the FYRST exit narrative. Support failure, not product failure, is the primary exit driver.
 No Kontokorrentkredit or overdraft pushes growing businesses toward competitors. No non-SEPA international transfers excludes internationally active users structurally. Limited legal form coverage (GbR, UG excluded). Product still maturing — missing roadmap features may prompt early adopters to switch.
early-stage gaps
ING's general customer service has poor responsiveness reviews — if the same culture applies to the business support channel, it will become a significant churn risk as the user base grows and encounters issues. No business-account-specific churn patterns documented yet due to product age.
