Stage 1
Awareness & consideration
How the player enters the Freiberufler's consideration set
 Tech-community word of mouth, expat networks, fintech comparison blogs. Consideration triggered by a specific pain point: FX charges on a foreign-currency invoice or a first non-Euro client.
mixed reach
Domestically focused Freiberufler rarely discover Revolut through German-language comparison searches, where German-first competitors rank higher. Discovered primarily by internationally active users. (documented: comparison platform rankings)
 Strong brand presence in Germany; expat communities; comparison platforms. Consideration driven by the free tier and the BaFin licence as a trust signal.
broad reach
Brand strength leads to high traffic, but the no-company-name constraint and missing tax tools mean a material share of discovered users self-select out during research. (documented: NeueBanken.de; Geschäftskonten24)
 German startup communities; word-of-mouth referrals; growing comparison platform presence. Consideration driven by the integrated invoicing-plus-banking proposition.
community pull
Organic discovery is limited outside the startup and tech-founder segment at this stage of market presence. Less visible than N26 or Revolut on broad Freiberufler searches.
 Comparison platforms ranking on "Geschäftskonto Freiberufler" queries; freelancer community forums; Steuerberater-adjacent content. Narrow focus gives high relevance per impression.
high relevance
Low general brand awareness outside the Freiberufler segment. Users who encounter Kontist without a tax-workflow need rarely proceed. (documented: NeueBanken.de; Geschäftskonten24)
 English-language Germany setup guides; expat freelancer communities; accounting software comparison pages citing DATEV and Lexware integrations.
niche reach
Visibility is limited outside expat and English-speaking Freiberufler communities. Rarely discovered through mainstream German-language comparison searches at national scale.
 Existing Vivid consumer user base converting to business accounts; comparison platforms; in-app content on freelancer registration guidance. Reaches users at day zero of self-employment registration.
conversion pull

Stage 2
Onboarding
Account opening, KYC, document requirements, first access
 Requires a registered company entity; director video KYC; proof of business activity. Sole traders using personal names directed to Revolut Pro. Typically 24–72 hours for incorporated businesses.
mixed
⚠ Critical Moment — Rejection without explanation: Applicants in certain business categories receive outright rejection with no reason given and no stated appeal path. For affected users, the journey ends here with no recourse. (documented: aggregated Trustpilot reviews)
 Under 10 minutes via mobile app with video identification. No minimum deposit. Straightforward for personal-name Freiberufler accounts.
positive
⚠ Critical Moment — Company name constraint surfaces at registration: Freiberufler discover the account and card will carry their personal name only, with no business name. For users expecting a professional business-facing account, this is a decisive limitation flagged by multiple comparison platforms. (documented: N26 support documentation; Geschäftskonten24)
 Fully digital, 10–15 minutes. Platform asks about company structure, expected transaction volumes, and business type to tailor the setup. Broad legal form coverage including GmbH and UG.
positive
Users report fewer onboarding rejection incidents than with Revolut or Airwallex, though no comparative data is available to quantify this. (documented: aggregated comparison platform reviews)
 Video-ident based, under 15 minutes. Does not require a tax number — account can be opened during the Gewerbeanmeldung process before Finanzamt registration is complete.
positive
⚠ Critical Moment — Tax-number-free opening: The ability to open an account before receiving a Steuernummer positions Kontist as the default choice for newly self-employed individuals in the registration window, before any competitor can reach them. (documented: Kontist product page)
 Digital, 10–15 minutes. Dedicated Gründerkonto enables GmbH/UG founders to deposit share capital before the commercial register extract is issued — a documented gap in most competitor offerings.
positive
⚠ Critical Moment — Gründerkonto for pre-registration companies: The ability to deposit share capital before commercial registration is a structural capability not offered by most neobanks. It positions Holvi as the default choice for GmbH/UG founders in the Gründungsphase. (documented: Holvi product page)
 Fully digital, approximately 10 minutes. In-app guidance for the Fragebogen zur steuerlichen Erfassung reaches users at the moment of self-employment registration.
positive
⚠ Critical Moment — Freelancer registration at day zero: Vivid accompanies users into the market from the moment of self-employment registration, not just from banking account opening — earlier in the lifecycle than any other player reviewed. (documented: Vivid product page)
Stage 3
First transaction
Initial UX impression and first value delivery
 First international transfer or FX conversion is consistently praised for speed, real-time exchange rate visibility, and cost relative to legacy banking. First domestic SEPA transfer provides no differentiation.
FX users: positive · domestic users: neutral
Users whose first transaction is a domestic SEPA transfer perceive no advantage over any other neobank. The product's differentiated value is invisible until an international payment occurs.
 Virtual Mastercard issued immediately upon account activation. First transfer or card payment is reported as seamless. Automatic transaction categorisation is noted positively.
positive
The "aha moment" at first transaction is primarily speed and simplicity — not a product-specific differentiator. Nothing in the first transaction experience communicates N26's unique strengths (BaFin licence, cashback). (documented: aggregated Trustpilot reviews)
 The invoicing tool is typically the first feature engaged — often before a bank transfer. The ability to create an invoice and see it matched against an incoming payment is the most consistently cited positive first experience.
positive
⚠ Critical Moment — Invoice-to-payment match: For Freiberufler who have previously managed invoices and banking separately, automatic reconciliation of an incoming payment against its corresponding invoice is the documented "aha moment" that drives early retention. (documented: Fintech Compass; aggregated App Store reviews)
 First incoming payment triggers automatic tax reserve calculation. Push notification shows the incoming amount, the estimated tax reserve, and the true disposable remainder.
positive
⚠ Critical Moment — Steuer-Topf activation: The real-time tax reserve notification is the most consistently cited positive feature across all Kontist reviews and is genuinely without equivalent in the market. For newly self-employed users anxious about tax obligations, this single moment is documented as the decisive retention anchor. (documented: Kontist product page; NeueBanken.de; aggregated App Store reviews)
 First invoice creation and SEPA payment. Incoming payments automatically matched to outstanding invoices by reference number. SEPA Instant transfers included at no extra charge across all plans.
positive
The invoicing match experience is similar to Finom's. The real-time VAT balance display — visible from the first transaction — is a genuinely differentiated daily signal for VAT-registered users. (documented: Holvi product page)
 Users who previously used a separate accounting tool report that VAT filing being accessible directly from the banking dashboard — without switching applications — is the primary satisfaction driver at first use.
positive
⚠ Critical Moment — Free-tier VAT filing: The availability of direct VAT return submission on the free Starter tier is documented as the most differentiating feature relative to other free-tier neobank offerings. No other free business account in this comparison includes this capability. (documented: Vivid product page; aggregated user reviews)
Stage 4
Daily use
Payments, receipts, sub-accounts, cards, notifications
 Multi-currency balance dashboard, real-time card controls, receipt attachment to transactions, team sub-accounts. Positively rated by internationally active users.
mixed
Friction is documented for Freiberufler whose business is primarily domestic: the FX infrastructure goes unused while German-specific features (tax reserves, invoicing, DATEV) are absent. (documented: German comparison platform reviews; Finanzfluss)
 Push notifications, automatic categorisation, and Spaces sub-accounts (paid tiers) are well-regarded in reviews. Mobile-first interface is clean and fast.
mixed
Absence of a desktop-optimised interface for bulk operations is a documented friction point for users managing higher transaction volumes. No sub-accounts on the free tier — requires upgrade to Smart (€4.90/month). (documented: aggregated Trustpilot DE reviews)
 Dashboard aggregates multiple bank accounts. Receipt scanning, categorisation, expense tracking embedded in daily workflow. AI accountant (January 2025) auto-reconciles invoices against transactions on higher tiers.
positive
The free Solo tier's five free SEPA transactions per month is a documented constraint — most active Freiberufler exceed this immediately and must upgrade. (documented: Finom pricing page)
 Purpose-built for income tracking and tax reserve management. Steuer-Topf updated with each transaction. Real-time disposable income display is the defining daily feature.
positive (core users)
Users who want a broader financial command centre find the feature set narrower than Finom or Vivid. No multi-banking aggregation. No team card management. (documented: NeueBanken.de comparison review)
 Real-time VAT balance visible on the dashboard. SEPA Instant at no extra charge. Business Credit Card available on Pro and Business tiers subject to credit approval — rare in the neobank segment.
positive
Absence of a standard overdraft means users cannot spend beyond their balance. The credit card option mitigates this only for approved users. (documented: Holvi product page; aggregated user reviews)
 Multi-IBAN sub-accounts (up to 12 on Starter), automatic tax reserve, real-time DATEV/sevDesk sync. Investment layer (stocks, ETFs, crypto) accessible from within the business account — unique in this comparison.
positive
Consumer-product history means some users initially treat the account as a personal finance tool rather than a primary business account — a framing Vivid's onboarding must actively correct. (documented: aggregated comparison platform reviews)
Stage 5
Tax & accounting workflow
DATEV, ELSTER, VAT returns, Steuerberater handoff
 No native DATEV export, no Umsatzsteuer pre-calculation, no ELSTER integration. CSV export only. Full tax workflow managed externally.
friction
⚠ Critical Moment — Steuerberater incompatibility: When a Freiberufler first engages a Steuerberater and is asked for DATEV-compatible data, Revolut cannot provide it directly. German comparison platforms consistently identify this as the decisive limitation for domestically focused Freiberufler. (documented: Finanzfluss; Geschäftskonten24; NeueBanken.de)
 No automated tax tools. CSV and PDF exports only. DATEV integration is officially listed as supported but technically unreliable — confirmed by documented user reports.
friction
⚠ Critical Moment — DATEV inconsistency discovered post-onboarding: Users who selected N26 partly on stated DATEV support discover post-onboarding that the integration does not function reliably for their account. No resolution path is documented. One Trustpilot review: "despite a prior confirmation, it is not possible to link with DATEV; for some customers it works and for others it doesn't." (documented: Trustpilot DE, N26 reviews)
 DATEV-format exports (MT940 DATEV and CSV) and native Lexoffice/sevDesk integrations. AI accountant reduces manual categorisation. No ELSTER integration for VAT filing.
mixed
⚠ Critical Moment — Lexware incompatibility: Lexware Office does not offer a direct bank connection with Finom, requiring a manual data export step for the 400,000+ Lexware-dependent users in Germany. (documented: Germanpedia accounting software comparison 2026)
 Plus tier (€25/month net): DATEV export, automated Umsatzsteuer-Voranmeldung via ELSTER, BWA reports, native Lexoffice/sevDesk integration with automatic booking proposals. Human Tax Service available from €79/month.
positive (Plus tier)
On the free and Start tiers, tax workflow support is minimal. The full value is locked behind the Plus tier. Documented structural gap: Lastschrift issuance is not supported — Freiberufler with recurring-billing clients must maintain a secondary account. (documented: Geschäftskonten24; Kontist support documentation)
 DATEV export and Lexware integration on Pro tier (€15/month) and above. E-invoicing in ZUGFeRD and XRechnung via Peppol network — implemented ahead of Germany's 2025 mandatory e-invoicing requirement.
positive (Pro tier)
⚠ Critical Moment — E-invoicing compliance: For Freiberufler invoicing B2B clients under the 2025 mandate requiring ZUGFeRD or XRechnung format, Holvi's native Peppol implementation removes a compliance burden most competitors have not yet resolved. (documented: Holvi product page; OMR Reviews)
 Umsatzsteuer-Voranmeldung filing directly to Finanzamt from the dashboard. EÜR tracking. Automated categorisation. Native DATEV, sevDesk, and Lexware integrations. All available on the free Starter tier.
positive
The free-tier tax workflow depth is the most complete in this comparison. The main limitation is that a certified Steuerberater review requires a paid add-on. No ELSTER-linked automated filing is documented as available for all legal forms. (documented: Vivid product page)
Stage 6
Support encounter
Channel availability, quality, language, response times
 In-app chat only for Basic and Grow tiers. No dedicated phone line on standard plans. German-language support available. Response times vary from minutes to days.
friction
⚠ Critical Moment — Account freeze with no escalation path: Account freezes triggered by automated compliance checks are documented across multiple Trustpilot reviews and Reddit threads. The critical failure is not the compliance check itself but the absence of a human escalation path on standard tiers, with users reporting weeks of inaccessible funds. (documented: aggregated Trustpilot DE reviews)
 In-app chat for free tier; phone support limited to Smart tier and above. German-language support available.
friction
⚠ Critical Moment — Account freeze with no escalation path: The most documented negative pattern in N26's German Trustpilot reviews. Weeks of inaccessible funds, no case continuity between chat agents. Several users reported intending to file BaFin complaints. (documented: aggregated Trustpilot DE reviews)
 Chat-based support during business hours in German and English. No significant cluster of account freeze complaints visible in public reviews during the research period.
mixed
No significant documented failure pattern distinct to Finom directly. Risk exposure remains via Solaris SE — though Finom ended its Solaris partnership in 2025, the transition period may carry residual risk. (documented: TechCrunch, June 2025)
 In-app chat, email, and telephone (Monday–Friday 9–17 Uhr). Support agents described as knowledgeable in user reviews.
mixed
⚠ Critical Moment — Solaris SE infrastructure failure: In December 2025, a Solaris SE internal error prevented Kontist customers from making any transfers for several days over Christmas. At least one user reported being unable to pay rent as a direct consequence. (documented: Financer.de reviews, December 2025)
 German and English support via in-app chat and email. Generally positive reviews for responsiveness relative to Revolut and N26.
mixed
A consistent pattern in Trustpilot reviews concerns card dispute handling specifically — users report that Holvi does not support them adequately when card payments are disputed, a process materially weaker than for fully licensed banks. (documented: aggregated Trustpilot reviews, including September 2025 review)
 In-app chat. Response times reported as generally adequate in user reviews. Post-pivot, some support agents more calibrated for personal account issues than business-specific queries.
mixed
The Solaris SE dependency means that infrastructure-level failures at Solaris would affect Vivid customers directly, regardless of Vivid's own support quality. (documented: Solaris SE BaFin fines 2024–2025; NeueBanken.de)
Stage 7
Growth trigger
Plan upgrades, team expansion, new feature adoption
 Upgrade from Basic to Grow (€25/month) triggered by hitting the €10,000/month free FX allowance or needing bulk payments. Solo Freiberufler very rarely progress past Basic.
limited upgrade path
The product's growth path is designed for scaling businesses with international payment volume — not for a solo Freiberufler whose main driver is tax compliance. Upgrade triggers are largely irrelevant to the core Freiberufler use case. (documented: Revolut pricing page)
 Upgrade from Standard to Smart (€4.90/month) triggered by the need for Spaces sub-accounts for tax reserve management. Go tier's Allianz insurance is the documented trigger for users with travel or equipment needs.
modest upgrade path
Few Freiberufler upgrade past Smart. The Metal tier's premium perks (cashback, travel insurance) serve a solopreneur profile that does not match most of the target segment. (documented: N26 pricing page and user reviews)
 Upgrade triggered by adding team members (additional cards and sub-IBANs) or reaching the free Solo tier's transaction limit (5 free SEPA transfers/month). AI accountant is a documented upgrade trigger at tax filing season.
clear upgrade path
The free Solo tier's five-transaction limit means most active Freiberufler must upgrade within weeks of account opening — a fast and predictable conversion trigger. (documented: Finom pricing page)
 Free → Start when monthly transactions exceed five. Start → Plus when engaging a DATEV-dependent Steuerberater or requiring automated ELSTER VAT filing.
clear upgrade path
The upgrade triggers are directly tied to real Freiberufler compliance events (Steuerberater engagement, first VAT return) — making the path from free to paid highly predictable. (documented: Kontist pricing page)
 Upgrade from Flex to Lite or Pro triggered by invoice volume growth, the need for DATEV functionality for a Steuerberater, or applying for the business credit card.
mixed
Credit card approval rejection rate is an undocumented but inferred friction point — users who upgrade specifically to access the credit card and are rejected may perceive the upgrade as delivering reduced value. (documented: Holvi pricing page)
 Adding team members beyond the Starter single-user limit, or needing the Smart tier's higher transaction limits and employee card management.
clear upgrade path
The free tier's tax filing completeness means there is no compliance-driven upgrade pressure for solo Freiberufler — the upgrade trigger is purely business growth. This is both a strength (low friction) and a monetisation challenge. (documented: Vivid pricing page)
Stage 8
Churn / Stay decision
Top retention drivers; documented churn triggers
 Stays: Internationally active users for whom FX cost savings are measurable retain the account — often as a secondary account for international receipts alongside a German-first primary account.
secondary account pattern
⚠ Churn trigger — Steuerberater incompatibility: Domestically focused Freiberufler who discover at their first Steuerberater appointment that the tax workflow gap requires significant manual overhead to bridge. (documented: German comparison platform reviews) No publicly available data identifies where these users go subsequently.
 Stays: Users who value the BaFin licence as a trust signal, the free tier's cashback, and the simplicity of the interface.
mixed
⚠ Churn triggers — three documented drivers: (1) Account freeze experience (documented: Trustpilot DE reviews); (2) DATEV integration inconsistency (documented: Trustpilot DE); (3) No-company-name constraint once users require a professionally presented business account (documented: Geschäftskonten24; NeueBanken.de). No publicly available data identifies where these users go subsequently.
 Stays: The integrated invoicing, banking, and accounting environment creates meaningful switching costs for users who have built their financial workflow within the platform.
positive retention
⚠ Churn trigger — SWIFT absence: The absence of SWIFT transfer capability is a hard product boundary for internationally active Freiberufler. (documented: Finom product page — SWIFT not supported) No publicly available data identifies where these users go subsequently.
 Stays: The Steuer-Topf feature creates documented retention — the real-time tax reserve automation is not replicated by any other banking product in the market.
strong retention (core users)

 Stays: Users who value the complete invoicing-banking integration, the bilingual interface, and Peppol e-invoicing retain the account.
mixed
