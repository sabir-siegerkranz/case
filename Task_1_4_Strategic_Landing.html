01 — Market sizing

2.63 million businesses.
One under-monetized wallet.

The target segment — businesses with 1–3 employees turning over €89K–€266K/year — generates a direct Geschäftskonto TAM of €400M–€920M. Including adjacent revenue pools, the full-stack wallet reaches €2.1B–€5.3B/year.

Five forces reshaping the market

01

E-invoicing mandate

Jan 2025: all B2B must use ZUGFeRD/XRechnung. Forcing active platform re-evaluation.

02

AI accounting

Finom, Vivid, Norman making Steuerberater-free filing viable. €1.8–2.5K/yr spend at risk.

03

Generational rotation

57% of owners 55+, 114K closures/yr. New founders avg. 34.4 yrs, 36% digital businesses.

04

Incumbent contraction

Sparkassen + Volksbanken below 1,000. Branches closing. Fees rising across the board.

05

ECB rate squeeze

Down to 2.0%. Neobank deposit-float economics compressing. Lending pivot or margin erosion.

Who they are

Professional/technical services and retail each 16% (~430K), construction 12% (~313K), gastronomy 7% (~181K). Digital-native persona: under-40, urban, IT/services. Second persona: ~773K+ self-employed with migration background.

How they bank

56% bank with a single institution. Loyalty held by Steuerberater workflow — 75%+ still use paper Pendelordner. 52% name Steuererklärungen as worst admin burden. Only 2% use open banking (vs 9.2% UK).

03 — Competitive landscape

Three tiers. No full-stack player.

The market has evolved into a complementary ecosystem. Players specialize and connect via PSD2, BaaS, and APIs rather than building across all dimensions.

Incumbents

Sparkasse, Commerzbank, ING, FYRST
Full-service Hausbank: branch access, cash handling, instant credit, EdB deposit insurance. 20-year relationships. Cannot build accounting or invoicing.

Neobanks

N26, Finom, Kontist, Holvi, Vivid, Revolut, Qonto
Digital-first at low cost. Racing to add workflow integration. Lack credit, cash infrastructure, reliable DATEV.

Specialized fintechs

Lexware Office, Accountable, SumUp
Workflow-first: accounting, tax, POS. Added Geschäftskonto as embedded feature. Cannot offer credit or cash.

04 — Gap analysis

What sole proprietors actually struggle with

Three friction points emerge consistently across KfW, VGSD, and Freelancer-Kompass data — each from the customer's perspective.

1
"I take cash from customers and I have nowhere to put it."

53% of POS transactions are cash. Construction (313K), retail (430K), and gastronomy (181K) = ~35% of TAM. Zero neobanks offer native cash deposits. Sole proprietors maintain a Sparkasse at €5–€20/month specifically for this.

2
"My client is 45 days late and my Krankenkasse won't wait."

30–90 day payment terms are standard. Dispo at Sparkasse: 11.6–11.7% avg. APR, up to 15.6%. At their neobank: no Dispo at all. Only Kontist offers €500–€3K embedded credit. Sole proprietors use the incumbent's Dispo because nothing else exists.

3
"Every quarter I lose a weekend to Umsatzsteuer."

8.7% of working time on bureaucracy = 11 hrs/month (KfW). 52% name tax returns as worst burden. 75%+ still use paper Pendelordner. Only 4 of 14 players offer integrated UStVA filing. This space is contested — Lexware, Accountable, Vivid, Finom all building here.

04.3 — Strategic recommendation

The cash deposit opportunity

76
%
of customer turnover flows
through other rails

Qonto's ~€42K turnover per customer against the segment average of ~€177K suggests approximately 24% wallet capture. The 76% flowing elsewhere goes primarily to incumbent Hausbanken — and for cash-handling businesses, it goes there because it physically has no alternative.

Why cash deposits

  • 53% of German POS transactions are still cash (ECB SPACE 2024)
  • Construction, retail, and gastronomy = ~35% of the 2.63M TAM
  • 0 neobanks, 0 fintechs offer native cash deposit facilities
  • N26's retail-partner model (1.5% fee, max €999/24h) is the only workaround
  • The dual-account pattern is sustained primarily by this single infrastructure gap

Why this captures market share

  • Cash-handling sole proprietors are structurally excluded from neobanks
  • Solving cash deposits converts ~35% of TAM from non-addressable to addressable
  • Every deposit = transaction + underwriting data + reason to move Lastschrift mandates
  • Incumbent networks contracting + fees rising = switching trigger

Effort needed

Not a product build — a partnership negotiation. The model is proven: N26 uses REWE, Penny, dm, Rossmann; ING launched retail cash across 12,500+ locations in 2024. Neither built physical infrastructure. The challenge is commercial (fee structure, settlement terms) not technical or regulatory. No banking licence change required. The playbook exists and the partner infrastructure is already live in Germany.
